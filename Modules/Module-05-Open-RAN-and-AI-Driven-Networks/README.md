# Module 5: Open RAN (ORAN) and AI-Driven Networks

## Overview

Module 5 introduced Open Radio Access Networks (Open RAN) and their role in modern telecommunications. I explored how Open RAN replaces traditional proprietary network architectures with open, flexible, and interoperable systems. Through the course materials and research articles, I learned how Artificial Intelligence improves network automation, resource management, and overall network performance within Open RAN environments.

---

# Learning Objectives

After completing this module, I was able to:

- Explain the purpose of Open RAN.
- Compare traditional RAN with Open RAN.
- Describe how AI supports Open RAN.
- Understand the role of edge computing.
- Explain vendor interoperability.
- Analyze current research involving Open RAN technologies.

# Research Highlights

The assigned readings demonstrated how Open RAN is transforming wireless communications through open standards, intelligent automation, and AI-driven decision making. I also learned how Mobile Edge Computing supports low-latency applications by processing information closer to the user instead of relying solely on centralized cloud resources.

---

# What I Learned

This module helped me understand why Open RAN is considered one of the most significant developments in next-generation telecommunications. I learned that open architectures increase flexibility, encourage innovation, and reduce dependence on a single equipment vendor. Artificial Intelligence further strengthens Open RAN by continuously monitoring network conditions, optimizing resource allocation, and improving network performance in real time.

---

# Key Takeaways

- Open RAN promotes open and interoperable network architecture.
- Artificial Intelligence enables intelligent network automation.
- Mobile Edge Computing reduces latency.
- Vendor interoperability increases flexibility and innovation.
- Open RAN plays an important role in future 5G and 6G networks.
